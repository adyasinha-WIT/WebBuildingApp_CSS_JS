Human Interaction Design (HCI)


Human-interaction lifecycle is a field that concerns how humans and computers interact to perform a goal. The software development life cycle (SDLC) guides the process of software engineering, and involves the structured development and maintenance of medium to large-scale software systems. Due to this structured approach, the SDLC constitutes some development activities that are followed according to a selected process model.

    The main aim of HCI is to make systems easier to use, so that people can achieve their goals faster, with less mistakes and greater satisfaction.

    The HCI lifecycle typically involves the following phases: -
  • Requirements gathering
  • Design
  • Development
  • Testing
  • Deployment
  • Maintenance
